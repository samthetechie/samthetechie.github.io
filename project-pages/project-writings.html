natural rock formations
Imperfection is the nature of form, otherwise all forms would be the same.		
		
To empathise with another being's suffering that suffering needs to be relatable.		
		                    
If difference can only be represented as identity, analogy, opposition or similitude, then representation reduces it to the difference between two elements, meaning it is reduced to a principle of comparison.		
		
Comparison is not only the thief of self-worth when we compare ourselves to other people but it could also be the basis on which we fail to appreciate the worth of an entity we find in nature, we are representing but not truly or fully observing it.		
		
Our empathy might not be extended to the perceived entity for lack of our ability to relate to it.		
		
For example I bend down outside and pickup a rock and at first, having not looked very closely, I perceive that it has a weight and texture and form but I failed to perceive that it was in any way alive so therefore I did not represent it as living and by comparison with myself as a living being and in order to be able work out how I might feel towards the rock I might make the comparison: I am alive but the rock is not alive and since we are fundamentally different I do not feel any empathy towards it.		
		
Due to the limits of perception of the smallest entity that I can perceive using the human eye, for example what appears to me to be at first a dense smooth irregularly shaped but otherwise inert entity, under a magnification lens, becomes a surface on which an entire ecosystem exists, consisting of small plants, insects, algae, microfungi, bacteria, viruses.		
		
If the rock is wet, water being so fundamental to living processes, there is likely to be an even higher chance that life is present.		
		
The mere act of disturbing the rock from the original context where it was encountered might have destroyed or otherwise permanently altered the habitat of millions of microscopic beings that I cannot, without technological enhancement of my senses, perceive.		
		
Reflecting that the human body is made up of mostly water and bacteria and suppose after a mineral analysis I discover that the rock has some iron deposits inside it, iron being only made in the heart of stars above a certain size, then both the rock and I are children of the same type of star, which is to say we are made up of some of the same stardust, suddenly the rock itself and the entire context in which I found it take on a radically new significance: the type and degree of representations that I can make about increases so much that I feel a degree of empathy towards it accompanied by mutual tolerance and respect for the other living beings and how they came to be and be in that place.		
		
Why should I interfere? Certainly not because of our differing forms, relative sizes and subtle or more apparent natures!
wood for the trees
While there are many strategies (adaptations) in organisms and many niches in living systems, the intervowen tapesty of electron transfer through the metabolic pathways or chemical processes of each form, life and living systems interlock.		
Where do humans fit in? We seem to keep chaging our minds. To all intents and purposes a city is a desert, a big clearing in the forest. In contrast, in a living ecosystem, like a forest, you need to understand the living systems around you to not damage them. For example after you have lived in a forest for a while, you understand that taking only the dead wood off the forest floor is like choosing to incinerate an entire insect and mycellium hotel, but you also might feel bad at hacking into a perfectly healthy living tree. So you are surrounded by trees but you cannot get any wood without destroying or killing. So you can wait, for example, for a large storm to shake and apply wind shear to the canopies of the trees and then there will be plenty of fallen branches and twigs that were 'ready' to be lost / are sacrificial from the point of view of the life cycle of the tree. Of course nothing is wasted in nature so taking the twigs rather than leaving them on the forest floor means you are denying some of the biomass to the soil which needs to constantly be fed in order to stay alive. Perhaps a compromise might be: after a storm and in order to clear the paths and ways that you already use to move through and around that forested area you can pick up all of the twigs and branches that are blocking the path. Obstructions to the path make it more unsafe to walk on in poor light conditions because you might trip on the branch, being conscious to leave other areas (off of the paths) completely undisturbed particularly that the life forms there can thrive and the soil remains uncompressed by the weight of your body acting through the small surface area of your foot. 'Walking lightly on the land' and indeed through life has a literal as well as a figurative meaning.

Things are more quiet than usual
you wake up again... but you were already 'awake', it's as if you have		
been operating at different levels of (un)consciousness for most of your life.		
Who was in control of the throttle? Why were you not taught that there was a throttle?		
For a brief moment you might have more CPU cycles available because you accidentally		
took a break from processing the past (depression) or the future		
(anxiety). In a flash you see the common thread: all of the gentle		
teachers in your life showed you what freedom means, what harmony means.		
What a beautiful way they have! So much inspiration. They are the		
gentle, empathic, caring and loving presence that has always been but		
never shouted over the noise to be heard. Never used force over you to meet		
their needs or those of others. Despite being the strongest (peaceful)		
warriors. How come the flowers and the butterflies were just outside the		
periphery of vision the whole time? Where they hiding or was I not		
seeing? To understand is to see. Sadly egos are attracted to bigger egos		
and someone who doesn't define themselves based on your approval (or		
not), will not fight to be heard / does not need that kind of		
validation. The most beautiful teachers can be found *everywhere* on the		
level of every-day experience but might be camouflaged- an adaptation to		
adverse-world, worse still they might have developed spikes and be quick		
to flight and slow to trust because of the impersonal brutal forces		
(invisible to ourselves because we never felt them) and to the pressure		
of predation. Can we understand each other's pain better now? Oh no.		
What did we do to ourselves? Can we see now why some make camouflage		
to hide? Why they might melt into the background? Others are eager to		
please just to form a mutual core (although never on equal terms and		
everybody looses as a result)		

So we see our developmental context was a mind-dominated culture and we		
specialised ourselves as human adjuncts to machines. The primary focus		
is efficiency and working to accumulate more 'knowledge', but wait: who		
wrote the books?

schools, the workplace and as citizens where does the violence come		
from? What kind of society would teach violence to children?

So how could we calibrate when we were lost at sea without a compass and		
all the maps and notes are written by violent war-makers? How to 'see'		
in terms other than 'enemy images'?		

How much fragmentation has occurred when the neurons in the heart were		
not involved in the most formative and critical parts of development		
during young childhood and adolescence? Critically: once you work out		
what happened, what does the road back look like?		

To avoid this waste of potential in the future: what about raising		
awareness of this phenomenon / developmental arc and having a bit of		
consensus about something like a tabula rasa for our children and their		
children (if they so choose)? Knowing that from this point we may see a		
highest possible future and letting the arrow fly after it has been		
fired, the arrow might see another highest possible future that we could		
never conceive of. So how would you best support them as a parent in the		
current context? What language will you teach them? The language of the		
heart (which they are born knowing and which we have mostly forgotten)		
or the language of the mind-dominated cultures which will 'best' prepare		
them for the world and it's sharp tongues which get sharper the more		
they are used?		

If not from birth then what is the 'age of self-determination or reason'		
for beginning to hone the kind of awareness and intentionality that is		
peaceful, knowing (with the heart) and present and leading to authentic		
self-expression and relating and right action given the current		
circumstance? Or do all attempts at some pre-conceived notion of success		
result in worse backlash, failure and counter-veiling forces? The brutal		
reality for some will then be continually searching an infinite fractal		
complexity and fragmentation of space and time with only a narrow beam		
of consciousness to light their way. If all you had was a narrow beam		
and every individual has a different internal nebula, what would be a		
meta strategy (a strategy to develop one's own strategy)? As many ways		
as there are reasons for being, never before seen in nature. One could		
try going back inside to find out who was asking the question. Then you		
realise that the lights will flicker on and off from time to time and it		
can be painful to be 'trapped' inside such a confining space.		
For example: how can you explain this to an old friend who had already		
moved on and forgotten your name, that you had wronged in the past when		
you didn't even know what you didn't know? So then comes the question:		
is this realisation a useful adaptation to present circumstances?		
Is it dangerous? Is it just a normal part of development / evolution?		
How do other people cope with this feeling and what are you once you		
stop defining yourself in reaction to things? Part of the solution		
might be to start wearing blank t-shirts instead of logos and symbols		
because ideas are just more excuses to separate I from I.
